Hello, my name is Tiffany Trieu and I am a 3rd year political science major at UCR who is on the route of graduating Spring of 2025. During my years at UCR, I served as a research assistant under Professor Ferrari who specializes in quantitative political methodology and comparative political behavior. As his research assistant, I conducted large-scale literature reviews on political attitude ambiguity, analyzing and translating findings into accessible summaries to enhance information usability. Furthermore, I also identified and evaluated research experiments, focusing on methodologies and arguments to aid project insights. To add to that, I also employed social science research coding techniques to systematically categorize raw data into a structured format suitable for analysis. Lastly, I met weekly with Professor Ferrari to discuss findings and refine research approaches, fostering professional development.

In 2024, I was a fellow of The Minority Serving Institution Research Academy (MSIRA) a program and research project run by UC Riverside political scientists Marissa Brookes, Kim Yi Dionne, and Jennifer Merolla. MSIRA is a research project that focuses on measuring the social and research impact of public engagement, or scholars communicating beyond academic audiences and sharing their expertise with a broader, more general audience. As a fellow, I received comprehensive training in research methods, enhancing analytical skills and proficiency. I also collaborated in diverse groups to complete weekly assignments addressing real-world political questions. Furthermore, I wrote detailed analyses of political science research articles weekly, demonstrating critical thinking. Lastly, I created and presented a board game project to teach process tracing, simplifying complex research methodologies to laymen.

Outside of school extracurricular activities, I also volunteered at the Inland Empire Ronald McDonald House. The Inland Empire Ronald McDonald House is a place that houses families while their children receive medical treatment from the Loma Linda Hospital or other nearby hospitals, providing them with shelter, food, and entertainment. As a volunteer, my responsibilities required me to maintain the cleanliness of common areas, ensuring a sanitary environment for all guests. I also was expected to collaborate with staff and volunteers to meet daily operational needs. Furthermore, I offered compassionate support to families with hospitalized children, fostering a welcoming atmosphere. Lastly, I organized family-friendly activities, enhancing the overall experience for visiting families.
